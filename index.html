The shortest distance from the pool to the two nearest lot lines, and to each structure, is drawn and labelled on the map. A line turns red when it is under its setback. Distances read to the nearest 0.05 m; type a value in the readout to nudge the pool. Many Quebec municipalities require 1.5 m; check your local bylaw.

Click each corner of your lot on the map, then Finish. Drag the green corners to adjust. The outline turns red and the distance line reports the gap when the pool is under the setback or sits outside the lot. Saved per location in this browser.

Outline the house and each accessory building (shed, garage), clicking Finish after each one. Draw as many as you need. Drag corners to adjust. The purple outline turns red and reports the gap when the pool is closer than the structure setback. Saved per location in this browser.

Use "Equipment set" for a single pin covering the pump, heat pump and filter together, or drop them individually. Drag into place. Each shows its distance to the nearest pool wall (min 1 m, the anti-climb rule) and, once a property line is drawn, to the nearest lot line (the property setback), turning red under either limit. Click a pin to remove it. Saved per location.

Mark the ladder, gate, or protected deck opening that satisfies the no-fence exemption. Drag to position; click a pin to remove it. Saved per location.

Stores the current shape, size, rotation, buffer and position so you can compare pool candidates. Saved in this browser only.

Get a free key at console.cloud.google.com (enable "Maps JavaScript API"). Adds Google Satellite and Hybrid to the layer switcher (top right of map). Sharp, but cannot be captured by the PNG export (browser cross-origin limit). Key is saved in this browser only.

Get a free token at mapbox.com (Account, Tokens). Adds a high-resolution Mapbox Satellite layer that, unlike Google, CAN be captured by the PNG export. Use this when Esri looks dated or low-res. Token is saved in this browser only.

Upload a survey or site plan image, then drag the pink handle to position it and use the sliders to scale it (match a known length, e.g. a 31 m property line), rotate and fade it over the satellite view. The white backing lays an adjustable white sheet over the whole view (behind the plan and drawings) so the plan lines stay legible: raise it toward 100% for a clean white page, or keep it low to still see the imagery. The pool draws on top. Saved per location in this browser.

Paste any XYZ or WMTS tile URL to add it to the layer switcher (top right). The Quebec orthophoto template is experimental and may be offline or access-restricted: confirm it loads before relying on it. Esri imagery stays the default.

In measure mode, click points on the map to check setbacks or verify imagery scale against a known dimension (e.g. your house wall). Export saves the current map, pool and distance lines as a PNG with a scale bar for a permit application (the uploaded site plan is not included; use Clean view plus a screenshot for that). Google imagery cannot be captured (browser limit); if Google is showing, the export uses your Mapbox layer when added, otherwise Esri.

Imagery: Esri World Imagery. Georeferencing is typically accurate to within about a metre at this zoom, but verify scale with the measure tool before trusting tight clearances. This is a planning aid, not a survey.
